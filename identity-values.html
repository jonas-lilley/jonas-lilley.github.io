Identity and values
An ethical decision
When I was 19 I moved into a flat with my friends as I started going to universtiy for architecture. At first it was great and we all got along very well, however this didn't last. Turns out being friends before hand doesn't mean that you'll make good flat mates. We all argued with each other all the time and I was struggling to adjust to university life and the challenges I was facing at home. Two of my flatamtes were a couple and they broke up. This is when I moved home for a week and spoke to my parents as well as outside friends for there perspective. After diliberating my own feelings I came to a descion to move out of the flat because it was the right move for me and my goals. It was a hard convosation and some feelings were hrut. At the end of it though everyone agreed that it was the best descion to all move out and go our seperate ways. I reflect on this descion rarely as I still beleive it was the right idea for me and everyone else. The situations before that though could've been different if it was me today, but you can't change the past only learn and move forward.
My culture and the people around me who have influenced my values and identity
I grew up in England without any extended family. It was just me, my sister and my parents. A few times we would have family travel from New Zealand visit but that didn't happen often. So my family is very close because we were all we had. I believe this has instilled a value of keeping a smaller circle, but you give and get a lot of love and support from that circle. Quality over quainty perhaps. I also grew up with parents and a community that treated me with empathy, and thats a privilage I won't ever forget. So I try to treat anyone, even strangers with that same empathy and understanding.
My strengths
Typically I've found throughout my learning life I see concepts from an orthodox perspective. This usually means I pick up on new ideas being taught pretty quickly which is a bonus. For better or worse I have got an obbessive or passionate personailty who really likes getting good at stuff. This causes me to be a hard worker no matter if I want to be one or not. Like when I used to work at New World in the produce section; on my off days if I found myself in another supermarket I found myself internally taking notes from other stores and thinking about differnt ways to do my job. I'm still not sure if that was a conscious descion.
My limitations
I have gotten hit with the A.D.H.D stick pretty hard. This causes me to have: a hard time starting and finishing boring or tedious tasks , paying attention to people, an inflamed reaction to positive or negative events and thinking long term. They all affect my learning jounrey negatively and on paper its not fair. However that is not an excuse for me to give up. I have developed systems and habits to work around and with these limitations. For the bad habits I haven't brocken just yet, I just have to keep trying. You only fail when you give up.
Working with others in a tough enviroment
While working as a landscaper the projects I worked on were on big commerical sites. We worked to a deadline and this specific instances happened on a friday. By the end of the day we needed to have finished moving the remaining soil into the gardens because in the weekend potential buyers were coming to view the properties. The guy I was working with was around the same age and we got along fine. It was only us two left on site and at the rate we were collectively working we were not going to make the deadline. So I told him that we needed to hurry up . He did not take this well and got mad that I was telling what to do, arguing that I'm not his manager. We did talk it out but only after arguing. If it was the me today in the same situation I wouldn't start off the dialog with a demand but I would start it as a question. So that he doesn't feel like I'm treating him as inferior but as an equal.

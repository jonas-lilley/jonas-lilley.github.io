With the ongoing development of ai and its inevivitable impact on how society opperates I am aiming to be in a position to take advantage of its forward directory before the ship sails for good. After completing the devacademy course and getting experience of working as a junior fullstack developer I would like to transition to working primairily on backend development. Then from there, evaluate an area of backend development that I overlaps with my intrests and has some scarcity. Grow my proficiency in python and other backend languages then try to transition into an AI/ML role.

How I will manage my workload

How I will manage myself and my workload can be broken down into three seperate systems to keep me happy and on track.

Exercise

Currently I have a peronal self-imposed non-negotionable routine of running and gym workouts. This helps me manage my anxieties and providies me with a space to see my problems from a different place.

Planning

I have: a daily journal, a daily planning sheet, a whiteboard and a phyical calender. All of these tools I use to schudule time to work on specific things in advance so I don't have to think about what i am doing at what time.

Every night I review what I have planned for next day and either leave it or change it depending on how the day went. I also have Sunday Morning as my time during the week to lay the planning ground work.

Support

Firstly I have my parents to rely on for emotional support if I feel overwhelmed. Then I have a close group of friends that I make to time to catch up with every week; whether its going for a beer or going for a hike.

How I will ask for help

In foundations I will reach out via discord if I am struggling with course content. A message which is descriptive of the problem that I am facing with an attached photo of the problem. Either posted directly to my facilitator, foundations-helpdesk or my homegroup.

What I expect of the Facilitation team

To be a respectful and helpful guiding hand to assist with anything I dont understand or have trouble with. To point me in the right direction, not lead me the whole way.
